Key Takeaway: In all 6 major metro areas, homeownership is out of reach for an electrician earning the median salary — median home values range from $498K (Sacramento) to $1.38M (San Jose), far exceeding the ~$265K-$333K an electrician can typically afford. Renting is borderline: affordable in Sacramento (27% of income), LA (30%), and Riverside (29%), but cost-burdened in San Francisco (31%), San Diego (34%), and San Jose (36%). Riverside-San Bernardino offers the best balance — lowest rent, highest homeownership rate (65%), and the most available inventory. Electricians who do own homes likely purchased years ago, have dual incomes, or live in less expensive pockets within these metros.
