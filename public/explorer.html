Fund Thesis

Invest in multifamily properties where renters have durable, AI-proof jobs. Two-layer scoring model:

Investor View — Is this a good market?

Workforce resilience, employment diversification, vacancy trends, rent growth, supply pipeline, population growth. Markets where tenants keep paying rent regardless of tech disruption.

Ops View — Is this a good deal?

Mortgage maturity (motivated sellers), unit count (operational efficiency), Section 8 (guaranteed income), local vacancy. Acquisition targets within qualifying markets.

Combined: great deals in great markets. Sensitivity analysis stress-tests assumptions with 1000 Monte Carlo iterations.

Scoring Methodology

Properties scored on configurable 100-point scale across 6 signals. Use scenario presets or set custom weights in the config panel.

Mortgage Maturity — Properties with FHA loans maturing within 1-2 years score highest (motivated sellers). Based on HUD FHA insured mortgage data.
Vacancy Trend — Counties where vacancy rates are falling (tightening markets) score higher. Computed from Census ACS 2021 vs 2023 vacancy rates.
Rent/Cost Ratio — Counties where median rent exceeds median owner cost (ratio > 1.0) indicate Day 1 positive cash flow potential. Census B25064 / B25105.
Area Vacancy — Moderate vacancy (~5-8%) is optimal — enough turnover for new acquisitions but not so high it signals distress.
Pop Growth — Growing counties indicate expanding renter demand. Census B01003 population trends.
Workforce Resilience (AI-Resistant Index) — Measures what % of a county's workforce is in occupations resilient to both physical automation AND AI/LLM disruption. Combines two frameworks:
Frey & Osborne (2017) — Oxford study scoring 702 occupations on physical/software automation probability (0-1). Electricians: 0.15 risk. Retail cashiers: 0.97 risk.
Eloundou et al. (2023) — OpenAI/UPenn study scoring occupations on LLM/AI exposure. Key insight: AI risk is inverted from automation risk — white collar jobs (analysts, writers) are MORE exposed to AI than blue collar (trades, healthcare).
Combined 50/50 into a per-county index using Census C24010 occupation data. Counties with high concentrations of construction trades, healthcare, protective services, and education score highest.
