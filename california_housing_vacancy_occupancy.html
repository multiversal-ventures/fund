Key Findings: California has 14.5 million housing units with an overall vacancy rate of 7.6%. The rental vacancy rate is just 4.1% statewide, indicating a very tight rental market. 44.2% of occupied homes are renter-occupied — well above the national average (~34%). The tightest markets are in the Bay Area suburbs (Contra Costa at 3.5%) and Central Valley (Stanislaus, Sacramento). The highest vacancy counties (Alpine 70%, Mono 60%, Plumas 46%) are mountain/resort areas where most vacancies are seasonal/recreational homes. San Francisco stands out as a major urban county with both a high overall vacancy rate (11.9%) and a high rental vacancy rate (6.1%), reflecting post-pandemic shifts and new construction.
